ECON1626 · AI Future Forecast · Australia · 2027–2030

Australia stands to gain $142 billion — but only if policy keeps pace with AI.

Artificial intelligence is already reshaping Australia's labour market, productivity trajectory, and industrial structure. This forecast argues that by 2030, AI could add up to $142 billion annually to Australia's GDP — but the distribution of those gains, and the fate of up to 1.3 million workers facing role transitions, hinges critically on the speed and quality of government and industry response. These projections carry genuine uncertainty: capability timelines, firm adoption rates, and structural labour market shifts are all difficult to pin down. This essay surfaces those gaps explicitly throughout rather than presenting false precision.
